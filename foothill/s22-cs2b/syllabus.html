Homeworks

You'll have a new homework every week. Homework will involve writing code and reviewing other students' code.

All coding assignments will be submitted to gradeoven.com. Grade Oven will compile and run your code, then give you an instant grade. You can submit as many times as you like. Make sure to save your code because Grade Oven only keeps your last submission! Details on how to login will be in your email and/or on Canvas.

Your code will be reviewed publicly. If you don't want me or other students to know which code is yours, remove your name and other identifiable information from your code!

Quizzes

There will be weekly quizzes on Canvas. Most questions will be open answer questions, not multiple choice questions.

You may retry each quiz as many times as you want but I will not share answers until after the due date.

Grade Information

Your lowest homework score will not be counted. Your lowest quiz score will not be counted.

Late Policy

The late policy is the same for homeworks and quizzes.

You get all the points that you earned before the due date. Iff you schedule office hours with me, you can retry the assignment as many times as you want after the due date and keep half of the new points you earned.

For example, if you get 60 points before the due date, then (after the due date) you get 100 points and you schedule office hours with me about this assignment, you will get 60 + (100 - 60) / 2 == 80 points for that assignment.

For example, if you get 73 points before the due date, then (after the due date) you get 92 points and you schedule office hours with me about this assignment, you will get 73 + (92 - 73) / 2 == 82.5 points for that assignment.

Class Policies

Cheating and Plagiarism

Please discuss all assignments/homework where the whole class can see it, in the Canvas forums or on the Discord server.

Cite your sources. It's common for professionals and amateurs to copy code from the internet. Copying code without citing your sources is cheating. A simple comment with a URL and date in it are good enough! If you're not copying code directly but got some ideas from a video, website, or book, please cite that too! It's a good way to share ideas.

Don't cheat. Reporting cheating is tedious. Writing negative letters of recommendation is less tedious.
